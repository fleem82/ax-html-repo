제조 AX Report [2025-06-02]



본 제조 AX Report는 최근 발표된 주요 기술 동향 및 분석 자료를 요약하여, 제조 산업의 AI 전환에 대한 핵심적인 시사점을 제공합니다. 아래 각 기사는 제조 혁신과 관련된 AI의 역할, 도전 과제, 그리고 미래 전망을 다루고 있습니다.




AI, 제약 산업 전반의 혁신 동력으로 부상



제약 및 의료 분야에서 인공지능(AI)은 약물 개발 가속화, 효율성 증대, 비용 절감을 통해 혁신을 주도하고 있습니다. AI 시스템은 약물 설계부터 품질 보증, 생산 간소화, 진단, 맞춤형 치료, 의료 기록 관리에 이르기까지 광범위하게 활용됩니다. 특히, 스마트 제조는 AI의 주요 미래 응용 분야로 주목받고 있으며, 생산 공정 최적화 및 품질 관리에 기여할 잠재력이 큽니다.



핵심 포인트:
AI는 신약 개발, 임상 연구, 제조, 규제 프로세스 및 시판 후 감시 등 제약 산업의 전체 가치 사슬에 걸쳐 혁신을 이끌고 있습니다. 심층 신경망(DNN)은 화합물 속성 예측, 신규 분자 설계, 독성 예측 등을 통해 신약 개발을 가속화합니다. 제조 분야에서는 장비 결함 예측, 품질 보증, 생산 일정 최적화, 연속 제조 공정 개선 등에 AI가 활용되어 효율성과 안전성을 높입니다. 또한, 공급망 관리, 물류, 수요 예측을 향상시켜 제조 운영의 스마트화를 지원합니다.



시사점:
제조업 관점에서 AI는 생산성 향상, 비용 절감, 품질 관리 강화의 핵심 도구입니다. 특히 제약 분야의 스마트 팩토리 구현은 AI 기술의 적극적인 도입이 필수적이며, 이는 장비 예지보전, 공정 자동화, 실시간 품질 모니터링을 가능하게 합니다. 다만, AI 도입에 따른 윤리적 고려, 데이터 프라이버시, 규제 문제 및 숙련된 인력 확보는 성공적인 AX 전환을 위한 선결 과제입니다.

AI 신약 개발의 특허 전략과 R&D 생태계 영향



AI를 활용한 신약 개발이 가속화되면서, AI 생성 약물 후보 물질의 조기 특허 출원 경향에 대한 우려가 제기되고 있습니다. 생체 내 검증이 충분하지 않은 상태에서의 특허 출원은 자칫 혁신을 저해하고 특허 장벽을 형성할 수 있다는 지적입니다.



핵심 포인트:
미국 연구팀의 '사이언스' 발표에 따르면, AI 기반 신약 개발 회사들은 기존 회사에 비해 생체 내 검증이 제한적인 저분자 화합물에 대한 특허를 더 많이 출원하는 경향(23% 대 47%)을 보입니다. 이는 철저한 검증이 부족한 특허로 인해 후속 연구 개발 투자가 위축될 수 있음을 시사합니다.



시사점:
제조업, 특히 첨단 기술 기반 산업에서 R&D 결과물의 지식재산권 확보는 중요하지만, AI 생성 발명에 대한 성급한 특허 출원은 기술의 실질적인 산업 적용 가능성을 충분히 검토하지 못할 위험이 있습니다. AI 기반 제조 혁신 과정에서도 개발된 기술의 완성도와 실현 가능성을 면밀히 검토하고, 적절한 시점에 특허를 확보하여 R&D 생태계의 건전성을 유지하는 전략이 필요합니다. 특허 심사 과정에서의 엄격한 기준 적용 또한 중요합니다.

AI Emerges as a Driving Force for Innovation Across the Pharmaceutical Industry



In the pharmaceutical and healthcare sectors, Artificial Intelligence (AI) is driving innovation by accelerating drug development, increasing efficiency, and reducing costs. AI systems are widely utilized, from drug design to quality assurance, production streamlining, diagnosis, personalized treatment, and medical record management. Notably, smart manufacturing is gaining attention as a key future application area for AI, with significant potential to contribute to production process optimization and quality control.



Key Points:
AI is driving innovation across the entire pharmaceutical industry value chain, including drug discovery, clinical research, manufacturing, regulatory processes, and post-market surveillance. Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) accelerate drug development by predicting compound properties, designing novel molecules, and forecasting toxicity. In manufacturing, AI is used for equipment defect prediction, quality assurance, production scheduling optimization, and continuous manufacturing process improvement, enhancing efficiency and safety. Furthermore, it supports the smart transformation of manufacturing operations by improving supply chain management, logistics, and demand forecasting.



Implications:
From a manufacturing perspective, AI is a crucial tool for enhancing productivity, reducing costs, and strengthening quality control. The implementation of smart factories, especially in the pharmaceutical sector, necessitates the proactive adoption of AI technologies, enabling predictive maintenance, process automation, and real-time quality monitoring. However, ethical considerations, data privacy, regulatory issues, and securing skilled personnel are prerequisites for a successful AX transformation.

Patent Strategy in AI Drug Development and its Impact on the R&D Ecosystem



As AI-driven drug development accelerates, concerns are being raised about the trend of early patent applications for AI-generated drug candidates. Critics point out that filing patents without sufficient in vivo validation could stifle innovation and create patent barriers.



Key Points:
According to a 'Science' publication by a U.S. research team, AI-based drug development companies tend to file more patents for small molecule compounds with limited in vivo validation compared to traditional companies (47% vs. 23%). This suggests that patents lacking thorough validation could discourage subsequent research and development investments.



Implications:
While securing intellectual property rights for R&D outcomes is crucial in manufacturing, especially in advanced technology-based industries, premature patent filing for AI-generated inventions risks insufficient consideration of the technology's practical industrial applicability. In the process of AI-driven manufacturing innovation, it is essential to meticulously review the completeness and feasibility of developed technologies and to secure patents at an appropriate time to maintain a healthy R&D ecosystem. The application of strict criteria during the patent examination process is also important.

Accelerating Industrial Digital Transformation Through 5G, IoT, and AI Convergence



In May 2025, the convergence of 5G, IoT, and AI technologies is accelerating digital transformation across various industries, particularly in the Asia-Pacific region. Notable application cases are emerging in manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, and smart cities, contributing to increased productivity and operational efficiency.



Key Points:
Representative examples include Telus's plan to build its own Nvidia-based AI factory, Pegatron Indonesia's factory utilizing a 5G SA network for factory automation, Siemens-Microsoft's joint development of industrial AI models, and Hyundai Motor's AI and 5G-based smart factory operations. The expected increase in AI-related spending in the Asia-Pacific region and China's accelerating manufacturing automation (industrial robots, private 5G networks) further support this trend.



Implications:
AI Transformation (AX) in manufacturing generates even stronger synergies through its combination with 5G and IoT technologies. 5G's ultra-high-speed and ultra-low-latency communication enables real-time data collection and analysis, facilitating the establishment of AI-based smart factories, digital twins, and predictive maintenance systems. Manufacturing companies must actively evaluate these infrastructure technologies and formulate integration strategies with AI solutions to secure a competitive edge.

Implications:

China's Strengthening Manufacturing Competitiveness and Accelerating Automation



China is rapidly expanding its global share in the manufacturing sector, widening the gap with the United States. This is driven by its vast manufacturing workforce, dominance in raw material production, and, more recently, aggressive investment in manufacturing automation.



Key Points:
China's share of global manufacturing value added surged from less than 5% in the early 2000s to over 30% in the 2020s, while the U.S. share declined from 24% to less than 15% during the same period. China dominates the refining and production of raw materials for advanced weaponry (e.g., rare earths, graphite) and is also rapidly accelerating manufacturing automation. The number of industrial robots per 10,000 workers is approaching 500, and China is expected to have 3.5 times more private 5G networks than North America by 2028.



Implications:
China's strengthening manufacturing competitiveness presents significant challenges for domestic manufacturing companies in the global market. Specifically, manufacturing automation and smart factory transformation utilizing AI, robotics, and 5G are no longer options but matters of survival. Domestic manufacturing AX strategies must be recognized as crucial elements not only for productivity enhancement but also for actively responding to changes in the global competitive environment, and efforts in technology investment and talent development must be further accelerated.
