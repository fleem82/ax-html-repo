Energy Challenges in the AI Era: Solar+Storage and Nuclear ⚡

Solutions are being presented to address the soaring electricity demand of the AI industry, especially data centers, and decarbonization goals.

First, in the case of Korea, solar+storage hybrid power plants are emerging as an alternative to supply stable clean energy to AI data centers 24 hours a day. According to research based in the Incheon area, a 95% solar+storage system (5% grid backup) can supply power at a cost-competitive rate (88-99$/MWh) compared to current industrial electricity prices, requiring approximately 6.7MW of solar power and 26.1MWh of storage per 1MW continuous load. This can be combined with gas or grid backup in the long term, and the establishment of a rapid grid interconnection process is pointed out as an important task.

  • Reference: Solar+Storage for S. Korea's AI Edge
  • URL: https://gspp.berkeley.edu/assets/uploads/page/2025.5_Korea_flat_block_working_paper.pdf

Global tech companies are focusing on nuclear energy to cope with the massive energy consumption of AI. Google, Amazon, Microsoft, etc. are actively exploring advanced reactor technologies and signing power purchase agreements with existing nuclear power plants. Nuclear power has the advantages of lower carbon emissions than fossil fuels, consistent power supply regardless of weather, and relatively flexible site selection. Expanding nuclear infrastructure is important for the sustainable growth of AI, suggesting the potential for a mutually beneficial cooperation model for both technology companies and nuclear operators.

Workforce Development and Ecosystem Building in the AI Era 🧑‍🎓

The advancement of AI technology brings new opportunities along with the challenge of building workforce and ecosystems.

The US manufacturing industry is undertaking strategic workforce development to address the widening skills gap due to technological advancements and automation.
