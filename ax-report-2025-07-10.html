The EU AI Act and voluntary practice guidelines require pre-designing transparency, data governance, and human oversight when utilizing ‘high-risk’ AI, such as in smart factories and predictive maintenance. Non-compliance can result in penalties of up to 7% of global turnover or 35 million Euros. Domestic manufacturers should specify clauses for model transparency, copyright, and cybersecurity audits in contracts with general-purpose AI suppliers, and like in the cases of Airbus and Mercedes-Benz, proactively secure standardization and industry cooperation channels for regulatory compliance to minimize risks and trial and error.
