As AI is used in decision-making in various fields such as recruitment, healthcare, and criminal justice, various ethical challenges are emerging, including discrimination due to data and algorithm bias, lack of transparency due to the 'black box' nature of AI, the risk of privacy infringement by AI-based surveillance systems, and ambiguity in accountability for AI decisions. The potential for AI to be misused for the spread of false information is also an important ethical consideration.

In addition, through Human-in-the-loop and Human-over-the-loop collaboration between humans and AI, AI should be used as a decision-making support tool, but full autonomy (Human-out-of-the-loop) should be handled with caution due to accountability issues. The need for workforce retraining and building a social safety net to address job losses and economic disruption caused by AI is also raised. Ultimately, global cooperation between governments, companies, and researchers is essential to ensure transparency, accountability, and fairness in the development and application of AI.
