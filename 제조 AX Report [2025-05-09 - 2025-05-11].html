Manufacturing AX Report [2025-05-09 - 2025-05-11]

🚀 The Future of Manufacturing Depends on AI Transformation (AX): Latest Trends and Key Strategies

Recently, innovation through AI in the manufacturing industry, namely AI Transformation (AX), has become more important than ever. 🌟 AI is gaining attention as a key driver for creating new business value, going beyond improving productivity, reducing costs, and innovating quality. Today, we aim to examine the present and future of manufacturing AX based on various domestic and international information.

1. 🤖 AI is Transforming Manufacturing Sites Smartly: Core Technologies and Application Cases

AI is already being utilized in various ways in manufacturing sites, creating visible results.

  • The Rise of Smart Vision Technology: Smart vision technology, which integrates AI, machine learning, and deep learning, is emerging as the core of quality control, defect detection, and process monitoring. According to a survey, about 55% of domestic manufacturing companies are considering introducing smart vision, expecting productivity and quality improvement through real-time data analysis. However, high initial investment costs and a lack of specialized personnel remain challenges to be addressed.
    [Fact Sheet] Manufacturing Industry, "Smart Vision Introduction is Essential, Initial Costs and Lack of Specialized Personnel are Obstacles"

  • AI-Based Automation and Process Optimization: ABB has introduced the 'Flexley Mover', an autonomous mobile robot (AMR) equipped with 3D Visual SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) technology and AI learning algorithms. This allows robots to create and share maps of their workspace and precisely handle loads of up to 1,500 kg (within 10mm accuracy), presenting new possibilities for logistics and line supply automation.
    ABB Enhances Flexley Mover with Visual SLAM

  • Synergy of Digital Twins and AI: ChongKunDang, a domestic pharmaceutical company, has built a digital twin system integrating AI, big data, and automation technology in its Cheonan plant. This system monitors production and environmental data in real-time and predicts quality indicators, supporting the achievement of a target defect rate of 0.01% without extensive physical testing. This is a good example of how AI can maximize quality control and efficiency in pharmaceutical production.
    ChongKunDang's AI Pharma Manufacturing

  • Generative AI's Innovation in Manufacturing Engineering and Content Management: OpenText supports technical document management, interactive search, summarization, and multilingual translation through its AI-based content management solution 'Documentum CM for Engineering' in the engineering field. AI agents automate impact reviews, visualization, report generation, and CAD drawing searches, suggesting that engineers' work efficiency can be greatly improved. In addition, the emergence of new memory architectures such as Mem0 for maintaining the long-term memory of LLMs (Large Language Models) is expected to contribute to AI processing and utilizing more complex manufacturing-related information.
    OpenText: AI-Driven Content Management for Engineering
    Mem0: New Memory Architectures for LLMs

3. 🚧 Success of Manufacturing AX Depends on Overcoming Challenges and AI Governance

While the benefits of AI adoption are clear, there are many challenges to address and considerations to make.

  • Adoption Barriers and Data Issues: Many manufacturing companies face difficulties in AI adoption, such as high initial investment costs, a lack of specialized personnel, and technological gaps. In addition, the performance of AI models largely depends on the quality and quantity of data, so data shortages, biased data, and difficulties in verifying AI results can be major obstacles to AI adoption. In particular, in the chip manufacturing sector, quantifying the value of AI investment, the lack of explainability of AI decision-making, hindering collaboration between partners, and reluctance to share data are cited as problems.

  • AI Governance and Ethical Responsibility: As the use of AI expands, concerns about ethical and legal issues such as data security, privacy, algorithmic bias, and intellectual property infringement are also growing. To use AI safely and responsibly in the manufacturing sector, it is essential to establish a comprehensive AI governance framework that includes policies, monitoring, and security measures. It is necessary to ensure transparency, data privacy, accountability, and compliance with evolving AI-related regulations. As seen in the use of AI avatars in the fashion industry, the use of digital clones and avatars raises ethical issues such as portrait rights, consent, and compensation, which provide important implications for the use of digital twins in the manufacturing sector.
