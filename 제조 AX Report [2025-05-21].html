🏭 Manufacturing AX Report [2025-05-21] 🤖

Here's a summary of recent trends related to Manufacturing AX (AI Transformation) both domestically and internationally. Let's explore how AI technology is driving innovation in the manufacturing industry, and how related policies and research are progressing. 🧐

1. AI Regulatory and Policy Trends: A Tug-of-War Between Federal and State Governments, and Domestic Policies ⚖️

In the United States, discussions surrounding AI regulation are active. A coalition of civil rights and consumer protection organizations strongly opposes the federal government's proposal to preempt state and local AI laws (with a 10-year moratorium). They argue that this measure would undermine state-level protection efforts from potential harm caused by AI systems, citing biased medical decisions, inappropriate conversations with minors, and deepfakes as examples of AI harm. They believe that holding companies accountable for harmful algorithms is essential for innovation and safe AI adoption. Currently, two-thirds of U.S. states are considering AI-related legislation, and 50 state attorneys general have warned Congress about the potential risks of AI. There is great concern that broad federal preemption could invalidate key state laws protecting civil rights and children's privacy. 🚨

  • 🔗 Reference: AI Law Preemption Opposition Letter

In South Korea, the direction of the next government's AI policy is drawing attention. Presidential candidates are pledging to expand investment and develop infrastructure to make Korea a leading AI nation. However, there needs to be a concrete vision on how to address concerns about the impact of AI and robotics on the labor market, particularly job losses and increased inequality. Comprehensive strategies are needed, such as educational reforms to prepare for the future job market in the AI era, discussions on social safety nets like universal basic income, and the introduction of robot taxes. 🤔

  • 🔗 Reference: Korean Presidential Candidates' AI Policies

The Seoul Metropolitan Government is expanding and reorganizing the existing Seoul Digital Foundation into the 'Seoul AI Foundation' to oversee AI policies. Based on the 'AI Seoul 2025' plan, it aims to develop customized AI solutions, build global networks, innovate public services, and promote AI ethics. This reflects the local government's recognition of the importance of AI technology and its commitment to actively promote industrial and administrative innovation. 🚀

  • 🔗 Reference: Seoul Metropolitan Government Launches 'Seoul AI Foundation' to Oversee Artificial Intelligence Policies

💡 Implication: With the advancement of AI technology, establishing regulations and policies for responsible AI usage is emerging as an important task both domestically and internationally. In particular, as the adoption of AI accelerates in manufacturing sites, proactive discussions and preparations are needed to address various ethical and social issues that may arise. 🧐

2. Industry AI Adoption and Technology Development Trends: Accelerating Field Application ⚙️

The Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy (MOTIE) is actively promoting the 'K-On-Device AI Semiconductor Technology Development' project to strengthen the domestic AI semiconductor ecosystem. The goal is to develop customized AI semiconductors through cooperation between demand companies in the automotive, IoT/home appliance, robotics, and defense sectors, and fabless (semiconductor design) companies, and to pioneer new AI markets. Participation from major corporations such as LG Electronics and Hyundai Motor Company is expected to strengthen the supply chain and reduce dependence on foreign countries through win-win cooperation with domestic fabless companies. 🤝

  • 🔗 Reference: MOTIE Fully Promotes the Development of K-On-Device AI Semiconductors

AI adoption is also active in the shipbuilding sector. Altair and HD Hyundai Heavy Industries' Engine Machinery Research Institute have signed an MOU for joint development of AI-based eco-friendly ship engine technology. In response to increasingly stringent environmental regulations, they plan to optimize engine performance using simulation and AI, and develop technologies to improve predictive maintenance and safety. This shows that innovation through AI is becoming important even in traditional heavy industries. 🚢

  • 🔗 Reference: Altair Signs MOU with HD Hyundai Heavy Industries to Develop AI-Based Eco-Friendly Ship Engine Technology

Generative AI models tailored to manufacturing sites have also emerged. InterEx, a company specializing in industrial AI, has released 'GenX', the world's first open-source LLM specialized for injection molding manufacturing processes. Trained on actual manufacturing data, it supports specialized Q&A in Korean and English, and aims to accurately reflect the specific characteristics of industrial sites that general LLMs find difficult to capture. It plans to expand into mold and machining in the future, which could be an important milestone in the development of customized AI solutions for each manufacturing sector. ✨

  • 🔗 Reference: InterEx Unveils World's First Manufacturing-Specific Generative AI Model 'GenX' Open Source

AI is also bringing innovation to robot design methods. The research team of Dr. Kim Byung-chul at MIT has developed 'DiffuseBot', a robot design technology using a diffusion model, a type of generative AI. This technology solves the limitations of existing AI-based design methods in designing complex geometric structures, and showed a 41% improvement in jump performance compared to robots designed by humans. It presents a human-AI collaboration model in which humans design precision parts and AI generates various shapes, and suggests the possibility of expanding to various fields such as automobiles, furniture, and industrial parts in the future. 🤖✏️

  • 🔗 Reference: [Robotics] “Generative AI Designs Robots” — Kim Byung-chul, Researcher at MIT

💡 Implication: Manufacturing AX, including government-led AI semiconductor development, AI integration in traditional industries, LLM development specialized for manufacturing processes, and AI-based robot design, is now being rapidly applied to the field through concrete technologies and solutions. This is expected to lead to improved productivity, cost reduction, and creation of new value. 🎉

3. AI Technology Development and Ecosystem Expansion: Big Tech and Research Trends 🌐

NVIDIA is strengthening its role as an AI infrastructure company. At Computex 2025, CEO Jensen Huang introduced 'NVLink Fusion', a technology for exchanging data between semiconductors that supports the construction of customized AI systems. This is significant in that it goes beyond the development of AI chip technology to provide a foundation for other companies to develop their own AI solutions. 🚀

  • 🔗 Reference: Jensen Huang: “NVIDIA, AI Infrastructure Company… Plans to Sell AI Chip Communication Acceleration Technology”

Google is aggressively integrating AI into all of its services, including search, platform, cloud, and Android, and is expanding its market dominance. In particular, the high monthly active users of the Gemini app, the introduction of the advanced reasoning function 'Deep Think', and the expansion of AI agent functions have the potential to change the way AI is used across industries, including the manufacturing sector, along with the popularization of AI technology. However, antitrust issues remain a persistent challenge. 🔍

  • 🔗 Reference: Google's AI Integration Strategy

Research on how AI technology, especially large language models (LLMs), is changing the way scientific knowledge is produced is also noteworthy. An analysis dividing researchers into 'insiders (computer science researchers)' who are directly involved in the development of LLMs and 'outsiders (non-computer science researchers)' who apply LLMs to specific areas found that outsiders are actively using LLMs to engage in application-oriented interdisciplinary research and solve social responsibility issues. This suggests that LLMs are accelerating research activities such as literature synthesis, idea generation, and data analysis, and contributing to the creation of new research areas. 📚

  • 🔗 Reference: LLMs Reshape Scientific Knowledge Production

💡 Implication: The competition for AI technology leadership among big tech companies and the development of foundational technologies such as LLMs will further accelerate manufacturing AX. In particular, changes in scientific research methodology may lead to the development of more innovative AI solutions in the long term. 🚀

The AI transition in the manufacturing industry is only just beginning, but its pace of development and ripple effects are exceeding expectations. It seems important to consistently monitor related trends and seize opportunities. 🎯
