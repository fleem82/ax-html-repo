Recent publications provide interesting trends and prospects for AI transformation (AX) in the manufacturing sector. Here are the key takeaways and implications.

1. AI, the Core Engine of Manufacturing Innovation: Nvidia's Vision and Physical AI ğŸš€

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang likened AI to an industrial revolution, particularly predicting the advent of the 'Physical AI' era where robots create robots in the manufacturing sector. This signifies complete automation and intelligence of production sites, and Nvidia emphasizes its significant investment in building AI infrastructure for this purpose. Regarding job changes due to AI, while it will affect all occupations, it also suggests opportunities to bridge new skills gaps. AI utilization skills and the acquisition of new technologies are expected to become crucial for manufacturing workers.

Google DeepMind's AlphaEvolve uses large language models (LLM) to autonomously generate algorithms that surpass human-designed solutions, addressing real-world industrial problems. Cases like improving Google's data center task allocation software to increase available computing resources and enhancing matrix multiplication algorithm performance demonstrate AI's ability to optimize complex industrial processes and maximize efficiency. This means AI can directly contribute to solving various problems in manufacturing sites, such as yield improvement, process optimization, and energy efficiency.

3. Accelerating AI Adoption in Manufacturing Sites: Hanwha Robotics and Foxconn Cases ğŸ­

The advancements of domestic companies are also noteworthy. Hanwha Robotics showcased its technology in the South American market by introducing collaborative robots that perform precise tasks by tracking human hand movements. In particular, the motion tracking solution where a robot mimics the worker's movements in the automotive parts powder coating process is a good example of how AI and robot technology can replace on-site expertise and increase productivity. The news of on-site sales and supply contract signings for the AI-based 3D vision combined 'Smart Machine Tending Solution' heralds the spread of AI transformation in manufacturing.

Meanwhile, Foxconn's strong first-quarter performance as a global manufacturing company is analyzed to be the result of the surge in demand for AI servers. The record-breaking AI server sales demonstrate that the adoption of AI is accelerating in various industries, including manufacturing, requiring powerful computing power. This can be interpreted as a signal that investments for manufacturing AX are actively being made.

Discussions on building infrastructure and nurturing talent to support AI innovation are also active.
