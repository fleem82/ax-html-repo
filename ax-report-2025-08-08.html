This summary analyzes 10 recently published key technical documents to present core trends, opportunities, and challenges in manufacturing AI transformation.

The most prominent trend is process virtualization and system integration centered around 'Digital Twin' and 'Digital Thread'. This aims to resolve problems in advance by simulating processes (virtual commissioning) in a virtual environment, and to secure transparency across the entire value chain by connecting distributed data from systems like PLM, MES, and ERP.

Additionally, 'Predictive Maintenance (PdM)' and 'Predictive Quality Control (PQC)' have established themselves as the most practical AI application areas. Through vibration data analysis, automated vision inspection, and other methods, they predict equipment failures and quality defects in advance, minimizing downtime and maximizing yield.

Recently, the emergence of 'Generative AI' and 'manufacturing-specific AI models' is noteworthy. These technologies are opening up opportunities for 'Hyper-automation', going beyond existing automation, such as the intelligentization of collaborative robots, automated quotation, and the development of AI models trainable even with small amounts of data.

However, for successful AI adoption, the complexity of data integration and solving 'Small-Data' learning problems specific to the field are key challenges. Therefore, in the short term, it is necessary to focus on areas where clear ROI can be expected, such as predictive maintenance, and in the long term, a phased approach is required to establish data governance and move towards an enterprise-wide AI-based decision-making system.
