Although AI technology adoption is rapidly increasing, there is a complex pattern in which public trust in the technology is rather decreasing. According to a global study conducted by KPMG and the University of Melbourne on more than 48,000 people in 47 countries, more than half of respondents were wary of trusting AI systems. In particular, skepticism about the safety, security, and social impact of technology was greater than trust in technical capabilities themselves. This is especially true in developed countries. Despite expectations for improved efficiency and innovation that AI will bring, there are concerns about cybersecurity threats, the spread of misinformation, job losses, and the deterioration of human capabilities. In addition, 70% of respondents agree on the need for AI regulation, so establishing responsible AI governance and institutional safeguards to secure trust has emerged as an urgent task.
