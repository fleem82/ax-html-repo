This analysis aims to synthesize the latest trends related to AI Transformation (AX) in the manufacturing industry and derive key directions of change and insights.

As a result of the analysis, manufacturing AI is moving beyond traditional operational efficiency stages such as predictive maintenance, real-time process monitoring, and computer vision inspection, and is entering a phase that innovates the manufacturing paradigm itself. Notably, 'Physical AI,' where AI extends beyond software to interact with the physical world like actual robots and equipment, and the 'Full-stack' transformation, which provides the entire manufacturing process as a service, are emerging as key trends. This signifies an evolution beyond simple process optimization towards intelligent autonomous factories.

Furthermore, AI's application scope is rapidly expanding across the entire value chain (Value Chain), beyond the production floor, to include AI-based demand forecasting and supply chain digitalization. The emergence of advanced technologies such as Composite AI and Generative AI is expected to further accelerate these changes.

For domestic companies with strong manufacturing capabilities, the rise of Physical AI presents a significant opportunity. For a successful AI transformation, an integrated and strategic approach encompassing the entire value chain, beyond the fragmented introduction of individual technologies, will be essential.
