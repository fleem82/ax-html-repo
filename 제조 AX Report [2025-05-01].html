1. 🇺🇸 Revival of US Manufacturing with AX: Seeking Opportunities Amidst Geopolitical Challenges 🌍

Hadrian CEO Chris Power emphasized the need to revive US manufacturing using advanced technologies, especially robotics, machine learning, and AI, during his testimony before the US Congress. He argued that reindustrialization is urgent to address geopolitical, economic, and cultural risks, and that the US's manufacturing capabilities, which have declined due to past overseas relocation, reduced investment, and downsizing of the defense industry workforce, must be restored. Hadrian presented successful cases of adopting AX in defense and aerospace manufacturing to increase production speed and reduce costs, and urged policy support such as prioritizing advanced manufacturing in federal programs, strengthening domestic procurement, and passing related legislation. This is a strong message that the US must regain its industrial leadership by combining innovation and execution (agile, automation, digital factory, AI utilization, and skilled workforce development) to counter China's manufacturing dominance. 💪

3. 🧠 Development and Evaluation of AI Technology Itself 📊

Efforts to objectively evaluate the performance of generative AI models are also continuing. 4. 🌍 AI and Society: Potential to Solve Copyright and Population Issues ⚖️

The copyright issue of AI-generated content (AIGC) is causing legal and philosophical debates. In the *Li v Liu* case, the Beijing Internet Court in China recognized the copyright of images generated by Stable Diffusion and judged the user who entered the prompt as the author. This is a ruling that recognizes user creativity by viewing AI as a 'tool,' but it differs from the traditional view that copyright should be granted to 'human creations.' Various discussions are underway in China regarding the view of AI as a tool and the criteria for originality of AI-generated products, and the United States and other countries are taking a cautious stance on recognizing AIGC copyright due to concerns about AI autonomy. In the future, the legal status and attribution of rights for AI-generated products will continue to be important issues. 🤔

* *(Reference: AIGC Copyright in China: Li v Liu Case, https://lthj.qut.edu.au/article/download/3825/1580)*

This is an example of the potential for advanced technologies, including AI, to contribute to solving social problems. 🌱
