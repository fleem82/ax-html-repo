The copyright issue of AI-generated content (AIGC) is causing legal and philosophical debates. In the *Li v Liu* case, the Beijing Internet Court in China recognized the copyright of images generated by Stable Diffusion and judged the user who entered the prompt as the author. This is a ruling that recognizes user creativity by viewing AI as a 'tool,' but it differs from the traditional view that copyright should be granted to 'human creations.' Various discussions are underway in China regarding the view of AI as a tool and the criteria for originality of AI-generated products, and the United States and other countries are taking a cautious stance on recognizing AIGC copyright due to concerns about AI autonomy. In the future, the legal status and attribution of rights for AI-generated products will continue to be important issues. ğŸ¤”
