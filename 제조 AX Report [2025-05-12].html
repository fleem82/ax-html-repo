2. âš–ï¸ AI Adoption Effects: Recognize the Gap Between Expectations and Reality. ğŸ¯

A Danish NBER study provides important implications for the real effects of introducing AI chatbots. According to the study, while the usage rate of AI chatbots increased due to companies' active promotion of adoption (distribution of their own models, provision of education, etc.), the impact on economic indicators such as workers' income and working hours was still negligible, less than 1%. The average time savings effect was about 3%, and the effect of leading to wage increases was not clear. There was no significant change in employment, total wages, or employee retention rates even in workplaces with high AI chatbot usage. ğŸ“Š

This is a different result from the general perception that generative AI will bring about rapid changes in the labor market in the short term. Of course, AI chatbot users report benefits such as saving time, improving work quality, and increasing creativity, but the proportion of AI usage in actual work hours is about 5-6%, and in some cases, new work burdens are created (17%). ğŸ¤”

In addition, similar trends are appearing in the humanoid robot field. Investments are increasing to automate manufacturing and logistics sites, but experts say that the intelligence and common sense of current robots do not meet expectations, and widespread deployment in the field is premature. ğŸ¤–

These studies acknowledge the long-term potential of AI adoption, but show that a balanced view of short-term effects or current technology levels and realistic expectations are needed. ğŸ§
